Section 1

Model-Free RL — From DP to MC

DP needed the full model p(s′,r|s,a). MC throws that away and learns from episodes instead.

🔥 endsem hot
The Problem — DP Needs a Model You Don't Have
Dynamic Programming
📐
V(s) = Σaπ(a|s) Σs',r p(s',r|s,a) [r + γV(s')]
⚠️ Requires p(s',r|s,a) — the full model. Real environments don't give you this.
⟹
Monte Carlo
🎲
V(s) ≈ average(Gt) over episodes where St=s
✅ No model needed. Just run episodes, observe rewards, average.
Prof: "You will not get the complete model dynamics in real world always." — warehouse AMR robot example. Objects may or may not be where you expect, people come and go.
The Sampling Analogy — Heights of 10,000 Students
Model-Based (like DP)
Compute exact average:
μ = Σ heighti × P(heighti)
Need to know ALL 10,000 heights in advance
Result: exact, instant, requires full model
vs
Sampling (like MC)
Measure a sample of N students, average them.
N=10 → rough estimate. N=100 → better. N→∞ → converges to true mean.
Result: approximate, improves with N, needs NO prior model

Backup Diagrams — BFS (DP) vs DFS (MC)

Prof: "DP = one level of breadth-first search. MC = depth-first search — go deeper and deeper until terminal."

DP — Shallow/Wide (BFS)
From s, fan out ALL actions × ALL (s',r) weighted by p(s',r|s,a). Uses V(s') estimates at leaves — bootstrapping.
MC — Single Deep Path (DFS)
Follow ONE trajectory all the way to terminal T. Compute Gt = actual sum of rewards. No branching, no bootstrapping.
PE-PI Cycle Carries Over — But Starts from Nothing
Start
Arbitrary/random policy π
No model → start randomly
↓
Policy Evaluation (MC)
Run episodes under π
Average Gt → estimate V(s) or Q(s,a)
Same goal as DP's PE, different source
↓
Policy Improvement
π ← greedy/ε-greedy w.r.t. Q(s,a)
argmax Q — no model needed
↓
Converge toward π*
With exploration (ε-soft / GLIE)
Same arc as Policy Iteration — no model
⚠️ Common exam mistake: "Model-free" ≠ "Policy-free". MC absolutely has a policy π(a|s). What it lacks is the transition model p(s',r|s,a). The policy starts random and is refined — it is never absent.
⚡ Exam Alert — Model-Free vs Model-Based
Model-based (DP)
Has p(s',r|s,a). Computes vπ via Bellman. Full backups. Bootstraps.
Model-free (MC)
No p(s',r|s,a). Estimates vπ by averaging Gt from sampled episodes. Sample backups. No bootstrapping.
Bias / Variance
MC is unbiased (E[Gt]=vπ exactly) but high variance (Gt depends on full stochastic trajectory). Error ∝ σ/√M.
Episodes required
DP: never. MC: always — must complete episode before any update.

The Formulas

Return Gt
Gt = Rt+1 + γRt+2 + γ²Rt+3 + ···
Sum of all discounted future rewards from t to terminal T.
First-Visit MC Estimate
V(s) ← average( Gt : first visit to s, per episode )
One sample per episode. Samples are i.i.d. → clean LLN convergence.

Mistake 1 — double counting
Counting s's 2nd visit in same episode as another first-visit sample. Breaks i.i.d. assumption.
Mistake 2 — Gt direction
Gt = FUTURE rewards from t onward. Never past rewards. Use Gt = Rt+1 + γGt+1.

Section 3

MC Action Values — Q(s,a) & Why V(s) Fails

V(s) tells you how good a state is. Q(s,a) tells you which action is best — without a model.

🔥 endsem hot
Why V(s) Alone Can't Drive Control
exact exam Q
You have V(s) perfectly estimated
You know V(north neighbour) = 8.0
V(east neighbour) = 3.0
V(south neighbour) = 1.0
Which action do you take?
→
Problem: you need the model!
"Move north" leads to V=8.0 requires knowing p(north|s,action). That IS the transition model p(s',r|s,a) — exactly what MC threw away.
❌ V(s) alone is insufficient for model-free control.
To act greedily on V(s): π*(s) = argmaxa Σs',r p(s',r|s,a)[r + γV(s')] ← needs model!

To act greedily on Q(s,a): π*(s) = argmaxa Q(s,a) ← no model needed ✅
Prof (exact): "Your control talks about which actions are good in a state. Your value of the state is not giving you that information... Your policies' correctness is measured only by Q rather than V."

⚡ Exam Alert — Q(s,a) and Local Optimum
Exam Q (exact from transcript)
"Why is Vπ insufficient for control, even if estimated perfectly?" → V(s) needs p(s',r|s,a) to pick the best action. Q(s,a) gives it directly via argmax. No model needed.
Deterministic policy failure
"Starting with a deterministic policy, you will never reach an optimal policy because you're not giving chance for other actions." — Prof exact quote.
u,w,x,y,z trap
Once Q(w,E)=−1 < Q(w,W)=+1, greedy policy never selects E from w again. Q(w,E) is frozen. The path to y(+10) is permanently missed.
Common mistake
"Just act greedily on V(s)" — silently assumes you know the model. This contradicts the model-free premise.
Section 4

On-Policy ε-Soft MC Control

The fix for the local optimum trap — ensure every action always has a chance.

🔥 endsem hot
The Fix: ε-Soft Policies
π(a|s) ≥ ε / |A(s)|   for ALL a ∈ A(s)
ε-Greedy (most common ε-soft policy):
π(a|s) = 1 − ε + ε/|A(s)|   if a = A* (greedy action)
π(a|s) = ε/|A(s)|           if a ≠ A* (non-greedy)
⚠️ #1 exam mistake: greedy action gets 1−ε + ε/|A(s)| NOT just 1−ε. The greedy action ALSO gets its share of the ε pot.

GLIE — Greedy in the Limit with Infinite Exploration

Fixed ε > 0 → always slightly random → never truly optimal. GLIE: let εt → 0 as t → ∞, but slowly enough that every (s,a) is sampled infinitely often.

⚡ Exam Alert — ε-Soft MC Control
Greedy prob (CRITICAL)
π(A*|s) = 1 − ε + ε/|A(s)|
π(a|s) = ε/|A(s)| for a ≠ A*
Backward G accumulation
G ← γG + Rt+1
Process t = T−1 down to 0, starting G=0
First-visit check (control)
First visit = first occurrence of (St, At) PAIR, not just state.
On-policy meaning
Same ε-soft policy both generates episodes AND gets improved. "Learning to drive by driving yourself."
